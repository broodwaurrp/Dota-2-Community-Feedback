#MakeDotaGreatAgain #MDGA 

We represent a part of the CIS Dota 2 community and many players worldwide who care about the current state of the game. We want to share our concerns about how Dota 2 has been developing in recent years.

For us, Dota 2 is more than just a game — it’s a competitive space and an important part of our community. However, there are several ongoing issues that continue to affect the player experience and don’t seem to be addressed properly.

The behavior score system often feels inconsistent and unfair. Instead of improving matches, it sometimes discourages communication and doesn’t always reflect actual player behavior.

The ranked system also feels problematic. The gap between skill levels is too large, and climbing ranks can require an unreasonable amount of time, which can reduce motivation for many players.

Another serious concern is the presence of cheaters. Many players feel that anti-cheat measures are not strong enough, and that violations often go unpunished, which harms competitive integrity.

Recent updates are often seen as lacking clear direction. Some changes feel incomplete or disconnected from what the community actually needs.

Gameplay balance is another issue. The current meta is heavily centered around aura-based items, which limits variety. Matches tend to be slower and more focused on farming, and comeback mechanics can make games unnecessarily long.

There is also a noticeable lack of engaging in-game events, which previously helped maintain player interest.

On top of that, technical issues remain a concern — including bugs, server instability, and performance problems that directly affect gameplay.

We are raising these points not to criticize for the sake of it, but because we care about the future of Dota 2. We believe the game still has huge potential and hope to see more transparency, better communication, and meaningful improvements moving forward.
